Why technical architecture matters for SEO

A website that loads slowly, has poor heading structure, or lacks structured data creates friction at every stage of the SEO process. Search engines cannot rank what they cannot interpret reliably.

AI systems add another layer. They summarize and recommend businesses based on textual clarity, structured signals, and entity consistency. A technically weak site is harder to cite, summarize, or surface in recommendation contexts.

Building with good architecture from the start is lower cost than retrofitting a poorly structured site later. It also means your content and SEO work compounds on a solid foundation instead of fighting against it.

Common questions about web development

Do I need a completely new website?

Not always. We start with an audit of your current site. Many businesses can achieve significant SEO and performance improvements through targeted architecture and content changes before a full rebuild is needed.

Why do you use Next.js instead of WordPress?

Next.js delivers faster load times, cleaner rendering, better Core Web Vitals, and native support for structured data — all of which improve SEO and AI visibility. It avoids the bloat and plugin vulnerabilities common in template-based CMS platforms.

How long does a project take?

Scope determines timeline. A focused SEO-ready starter site can launch in two to four weeks. More complex multi-service sites typically require six to ten weeks.

Can you work with our existing design?

Yes. We can build around existing brand guidelines, apply architecture improvements over existing design systems, or develop a new design if that is the clearer path.

Do we need ongoing support after launch?

SEO improvement is iterative, so ongoing support is valuable. We offer monthly retainers for teams that want consistent iteration, and one-off audits for those working independently after launch.
